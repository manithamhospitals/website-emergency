Know The Signs

Do Not Wait If These Emergency Symptoms Appear

Some conditions can worsen in minutes. Early arrival helps our team assess quickly, stabilize sooner, and reduce the risk of complications.

Life-Threatening Symptoms

Immediate emergency support is strongly advised if a patient develops sudden or severe warning signs.

  • Chest pain, pressure, or suspected heart attack symptoms
  • Sudden breathlessness, wheezing, or severe asthma distress
  • Loss of consciousness, confusion, seizures, or collapse
  • Stroke symptoms such as facial droop, weakness, or speech difficulty

Urgent Injury Or Trauma

Accidents and injuries often need rapid evaluation even when the full damage is not obvious at first.

  • Road traffic injuries or major falls
  • Uncontrolled bleeding or deep wounds
  • Head injury, fractures, or severe limb pain
  • High fever with dehydration, persistent vomiting, or severe weakness
